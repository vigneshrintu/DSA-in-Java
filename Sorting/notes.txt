Types of vedas
->Overview of Vedas:

The Vedas are the oldest sacred texts of Hinduism, and they are considered the ultimate authority in Hindu philosophy and religious practice.
There are four primary Vedas: Rigveda, Yajurveda, Samaveda, and Atharvaveda. Each Veda consists of four parts - Samhitas, Brahmanas, Aranyakas, and Upanishads.
Rigveda:

Rigveda is the oldest Veda and consists of hymns dedicated to various deities.
It is considered the foundation for many rituals and mantras in Hinduism.
Yajurveda:

Yajurveda deals with the procedural aspects of rituals and sacrifices.
It provides instructions for the correct performance of ceremonies.
Samaveda:

Samaveda contains musical notations and melodies used in chanting Vedic hymns.
Atharvaveda:

Atharvaveda includes magical incantations, spells, and charms.
It deals with aspects of daily life, including healing, marriage, and more.
Samhitas, Brahmanas, Aranyakas, and Upanishads:

Samhitas are collections of hymns and mantras.
Brahmanas provide explanations and guidance on rituals and ceremonies.
Aranyakas serve as a bridge between rituals and philosophical discussions.
Upanishads are the philosophical and metaphysical portions, discussing concepts like the nature of the self (Atman) and the ultimate reality (Brahman).
